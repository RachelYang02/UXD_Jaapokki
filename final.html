FINAL REFINEMENT

Donatr: the final, polished product.


WHAT IS DONATR

Donatr is a web platform for facilitating collaborative donation. From the homepage, users can search whatever is relevant to their donation needs. The user is automatically presented with a list of donation centers relevant to their search, along with basic information about each result, including what items are accepted and the center’s address. Clicking on a result shows more detailed information about that specific donation center. From here, users have ideally found enough information to decide if they want to use this donation center and how to get there. Furthermore, users can also schedule a pickup from this page, which entails scheduling for a donation center employee or a volunteer to pick up their items for donating. Before scheduling a pickup, however, the user has to login. Before scheduling a pickup, users (who are not already logged in) are prompted to login. Users can also access the login page from anywhere on the app. Once logged in, users have access to their profile page, which provides a way to view the user’s donation history and any future scheduled pickups.


Donatr is unique. To our knowledge, there does not exist a similar product that serves both as a comprehensive database of local donation centers, detailing what goods donation centers can and cannot accept and other information about the organizations, and as a platform for donors to schedule pickups. Donatr saves donors time and energy, and makes donating goods a more accessible practice.

RATIONALE

In our Needs Analysis Report, we identified two key user groups based on their motivations; those who had to get rid of stuff because of a life change, and those who wanted to donate for the sake of helping others. Our first group of users were low on time, had limited access to transportation, and needed to donate specific items. Donatr fulfills all these needs: searching for donation centers that accept specific items is quick and easy, and finding a way to transport your goods is simplified by our volunteer/donation center pickup system. Our second group was willing to put more effort in, but also cared more about the causes their donations supported. Our application prominently displays information about the charities, and links these users to more information. It also allows them to give their time by volunteering to pickup other peoples’ goods, and displays their impact on their profile page.


Additionally, our users need Donatr to maintain their privacy and safety. As our interface allows for user-to-user communication, there are special considerations to be made with regard to who can volunteer to pick-up donations (for example, registered sex offenders might be precluded from registering as a volunteer) and how users schedule volunteer pickups (for example, profile photo-based systems, such as Uber, perpetuates racism through implicit bias). We have not had time to fully prototype and implement systems to ensure user privacy and safety, but this field would be immediately necessary to research in future work.



DESIGN DECISIONS

Some key design decisions are highlighted in our feature call-outs below.





We were forced to scope our prototype to fit our time constraints. In that process, for example, we chose to only implement the donor-side interaction, and did not prototype the volunteer-side interaction. Further simplifications are detailed in the Trade-Offs + Down-Scoping section of our Design Refinement report. As we moved into usability testing, we prioritized researching options for our search function, since it is highly visible (it lives on the homepage) and is critical to the overall experience. In the future, we would conduct further usability studies on other features, including the pickup scheduling page and the user profile.
